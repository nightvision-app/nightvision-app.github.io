# Night Vision Apps

Marketing page and App Store documentation for **Night Vision Apps for iPhone** — a small family of iOS apps that turn your phone into a real night vision device for low-light outdoor use.

Live site: [nightvision-app.github.io](https://nightvision-app.github.io/)

## The apps

### Green Night — Real Night Vision App

Free, no ads, no tracking. The classic green night vision look.

The app pushes the iPhone camera to maximum exposure and sensitivity to collect more photons from the scene. The result is the iconic green low-light image, useful outdoors at night when there's at least some ambient light to amplify.

- **App Store:** https://apps.apple.com/ua/app/green-night-night-vision-app/id1616351140
- **Price:** Free

### Pro Night — Professional Night Vision App

A more advanced night vision device in your smartphone. Real-time low-light enhancement powered by the phone's GPU.

Pro Night uses GPU-accelerated photon accumulation to extract a usable image from very low-light scenes. It renders the result in a white phosphor look with a cool teal tint — similar to modern NVG (night vision goggle) tubes. More modes and finer control compared to Green Night.

- **App Store:** https://apps.apple.com/ua/app/pro-night-night-vision-app/id1638753363
- **Price:** Paid

## How it works — and what to expect

Both apps work around the physical limits of a smartphone camera. They collect as many photons as possible from the scene, which means the image may refresh slowly in very low light. That is not lag — that is the physics of night vision on a phone.

Image quality depends on:
- Your specific iPhone model (sensor and ISP capabilities).
- The amount of ambient light available.

**Use the apps where they work:** outdoors at night with at least some light to amplify — a starry sky, moonlight, distant street light, etc.

**Where they won't work:**
- Complete darkness (a windowless room) — there are no photons to amplify.
- Daylight or bright light — the sensor oversaturates and the screen goes white.

How it works — and what to expect

Both apps work around the physical limits of a smartphone camera. They collect as many photons as possible from the scene, which means the image may refresh slowly in very low light. That is not lag — that is the physics of night vision on a phone.

Image quality depends on your specific iPhone model and on how much ambient light is available. Use the apps outdoors at night where there is at least some light to amplify, such as a starry sky or moonlight.

Do not expect a visible image in complete darkness, like a windowless room — there are no photons to amplify. And do not use the apps in daylight or bright light, where the sensor will be oversaturated and the screen will go white.
